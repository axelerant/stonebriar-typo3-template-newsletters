The Land of Eternal Spring. . .
by Tom Hayes

There are three words that will cause most of you to begin to sweat, just by reading them, Summer in Texas. Multiple 100 degree days, yards that are brown and dry, and ever increasing electric bills are phrases that come to mind in our heat induced delirium during the long months of summer.

When 18 individuals from Stonebriar left June 27 for a mission trip to Guatemala, which is south of us and therefore nearer to the equator, they expected it to be even hotter. However, working in the mountainous country of Guatemala, which is called “The Land of Eternal Spring” brought a respite from the Texas heat and also brought an even sweeter satisfaction at the opportunities that the Lord placed before our team.

Team members of varying ages and skills were completely unified and able to participate in a variety of ministries where they reached out to the people of Guatemala. Our team consisted of three main ministry areas:

Medical Clinic
The Stonebriar Team had three nurses and multiple volunteers who partnered with a team of medical students from the U.S. and Guatemalan doctors and dentists to see more than 600 patients in three days. They served men, women and children of all ages who were seeking to have their needs met at the clinic. There were four main components to the clinic: dental, vision, medical, and the pharmacy. Our team helped meet their physical needs, and also prayed with most of the patients, including several that accepted Christ.

Vacation Bible School
Our Stonebriar team led a VBS that had more than 500 children a day, with ten workers and three languages!!! After the first day, the local school officials cancelled the afternoon portion of school so all the children could participate. The stories, songs, and crafts were entertaining and all told the message that God loves these children. The rain and facilities were difficulties that did not deter the love or passion that our team showed these children.

Pastor’s Seminar Three Stonebriar pastors and one intern were able to lead twelve local church leaders through a three day seminar. Most of these local leaders had not received formalized training of any kind. Our team led them through a course called “Bible Study Methods” where we taught them the tools needed to study scripture and then prepare sermons on what they learned.

The unity and willingness of our Stonebriar team was evidence of the work of God in their lives, and He empowered them to show His love to each person they encountered in Guatemala.

Connecting With Lebanon

On Saturday, July 18, one of SCC’s Adult Fellowships (AF), 24 hours ’til Monday, had a party to celebrate and connect with Caleb and Nicolette Hutcherson, two of our missionaries in Beirut, Lebanon. The class “Skyped” with the Hutcherson’s to hear a first—hand update of their first year in Beirut. Skyping can be thought of as a video conference tool, so the AF and the Hutcherson’s were able to see each other as they interacted. They were able to hear about recent ministry opportunities, the process of learning Arabic, and upcoming plans.

The group also had the opportunity to pray for Caleb and Nicolette and their ministry. To make the connection even stronger, the Fellowship decided to try their hand at several traditional Lebanese foods. It was a social gathering, but one that had eternal purpose and impact.

Perspectives Offered at SCC This Fall
Have you ever wanted to know more about what the Bible teaches about missions? There will be a 14 week class offered this fall called Perspectives on the World Christian Movement. The class will be on Tuesday evenings, from 6:30 to 9:30 p.m., September 1 through December 8.

The class will be foundational and meaningful in your understanding of our great God, and will motivate you to be involved in reaching those who have not yet heard of the gospel of Christ. While the class is a significant commitment, you will thoroughly enjoy the worship, community, and varied teaching that will be present in every single class. There will be a different instructor each week which includes seminary professors, pastors, and professionals from the missions community, some of whom will be travelling from as far as California to teach our class. The class will be worth every penny and hour you devote to it.

If you have any questions, please email the Missions Department or to register, go to the Perspectives website here.
